Blog Post 4

As someone who almost exclusively uses dark themes for my browsers, IDEs, and word processing programs, I wanted to integrate a dark theme into my blog. I started looking at implementations such as the theme switcher references below but wanted to stick to a simpler approach. I started by changing the background color and finding appropriate font colors to make sure the text was still legible. After finding a color that I liked, I applied it to all of my headers and anchors for a cohesive theme. I then looked into finding a unique font from Google fonts and imported that as well.

After changing the color scheme and fonts I needed to redo the style of my code snippets. I found a template to start from on StackOverflow and altered the size and color scheme to match the page. Originally, I was going to add line numbers as well, but I was not able to get that working.

Sample code:


        @import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css?family=Allerta');
        body {
            font-family: "Allerta", "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;
            line-height: 1.7;
            font-size: 15px;
        
            letter-spacing: -0.3px;
            background-color: #2a2a2b;
            color: #CECECE;
        
            margin-left: 200px;
            margin-right: 200px;  
        }
